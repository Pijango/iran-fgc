Carbon Zero Exhaust

This is CZ Exhaust V2!

We started our research based on the 6 Project Sectors we had and after several brainstorming sessions we decided to choose the Transport Sector. According to our research, since 2010, more than 60% of Tehran’s air pollution is generated from transportation . This is caused due to low quality fuel, low quality cars, poor road and urban planning. We also need to consider the fact that the technology of electric cars is not going to be applied in our country in the near future. We redesigned the exhaust to be more compact.

Step One

There is a chamber with NaOH inside our exhaust, where gas enters through one side and dissolves the CO2, transforming it into sodium carbonate precipitation.

Step Two

There is an electrostatic charging via electrodes to particles and then through the way particles are moving, they end up being absorbed into the filters inside the device.

Step Three

Passing through a magnetic field and moving in a circular path where they end up sticking to the filters that are placed inside.

Step Four

Electrical charged particles are dangerous and may stick to human lungs, therefore, after step three, all remaining particles are processed through HEPA filters.

Step Five

Finally, the smoke passes from containing a graphene nanoparticle metal foam box, which is placed inside in the trunk of the car and It is possible to replace the box and foams inside the car trunk after a specific period of time.

Conclusion

We used solidworks to redesign it and with a help of an expert we 3d printed our new design.
The Problem is Filters capacity and Replacement of used filters. And the Solution is Using a mechanical exchange system with a sensor to automatically exchange filters and passing through the NaOH solution. We will continue to do so and fully develop the process to achieve our main goals, even after First Global. We are in the process of researching the conversion of captured carbon into Biochar, which is a soil conditioner and stable storage of carbon.

1.What happens to captured carbons ?

We want to transport captured carbon underground in a special reservards until it transforms to fossil fuel in the near future . We are also trying to find a way to transform the carbon dioxide into “biochar“ that can be used as a feed supplement for animal farming.

2. How often should the filters be replaced?

We need some data and experiments to find out the exact answer for this question but we designed a Revolver filter changing system that prolongs the filter replacement procedure.

we try to have an estimation about the price and it would be about $400 to $600, but we need to do experiments and research to find out how much it costs depending on the material that we use in this project
