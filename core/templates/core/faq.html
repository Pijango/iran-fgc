1.Who are we? ¶

We represent Iran in the FIRST Global Challenge robotics competition. We have participated in this competition Every Year, since 2017, the first round of the competition.

2. What is FIRST Global Challenge? ¶

The FIRST Global Challenge is an olympics-style, international robotics competition that takes place in a different country each year. FIRST Global invites each nation to send a team to build and program a robot to compete. Teams work together to complete tasks in a game themed around one of the greatest challenges facing our planet, including the 14 Grand Challenges for Engineering, in an effort to foster understanding and cooperation among the youth of the world as they use their abilities to solve the world’s problems. The challenges we face as a global society need to be solved, and the next generation can meet the task — together.

5. FGC 2022 - Geneva ¶

Join us from 13-16 October 2022 in Geneva, Switzerland! More than 180 countries will come together face-to-face in the spirit of global purpose, unity, and collaboration. There is no more appropriate place for this year’s FIRST Global Challenge than Geneva, the “Capital of Peace” where nations gather to solve diplomatic challenges and where scientific innovation flourishes.

3. How to support us? ¶

You can support this team both financially and spiritually. Your spiritual support can be in the form of holding some additional courses (if you have particular expertise that is suitable for this age group), providing a training place, workshop or hall.
You can also volunteer to help in the form of technical and executive staff based on time.
Your financial support can also be in different ways, including paying cash contributions to cover some expenses of the team inside or outside Iran, becoming a sponsor in the team, paying for face-to-face and online classes, and paying for coaches' teaching fees, and similar cases.

If you are willing to help us, please Contact Us

4. How to participate? ¶

After announcing the public call to send resumes to the team's official email, the examination and selection of students will begin in June. According to the policy of this year's executive team, the main members of the team are 15 students. Finally, five students will represent the Iranian team in the final four days of the competition, along with the head coach and the team manager. It should be mentioned that in the end, a certificate of participation in the competition will be issued to all 15 students, coaches, executives, and the primary staff of the team.

Contact Us if you think you can use the opportunity!
